# Monday (13/04/2026)

**Setup**
- Equipment: Rack, barbell, plates, wall ball, dumbbell or KB (40/25 kg)
- Timing: 60 min hard cap
- Stimulus: Back squat strength peaking to a heavy single, then
  an alternating couplet EMOM that keeps legs and core
  working while the barbell cools.
- Scaling A: Keep percentages. Wall balls to lighter ball.
  OH lunges at 25/15 kg. V-ups to sit-ups.
- Scaling B: Reduce squat load 5-10% across all sets. Push-ups
  to knee push-ups. OH lunges to front rack KB lunges.
- Logistics: One rack per athlete. OH lunge, wall ball, and push-up
  stations staged before the strength block starts.
- Top Cues: "Brace before you sit." "Drive through the floor."
  "Keep the ball on target."

## Warmup (10 min)
- **R1 RAISE (2:00 easy):** Shuttle runs or easy bike flush
  - 8 x Squat-to-stand
  - 8 x Glute bridges
- **R2 ACTIVATE (1:30 moderate):** Lateral shuffle + direction changes
  - 6 x Tempo air squats
  - 6 x Alternating reverse lunges
- **R3 POTENTIATE (1:00 hard):** 2 x 15s acceleration shuttles
  - 4 x Jump squats
  - 5 x Push-ups
- Rest 30s between rounds

## Post-Warmup Mobility (90s)
- Hip flexor stretch: 45s/side
- Deep squat hold: 45s

## Strength: Back Squat (14 min)
**Every 2:00 for 7 sets:**

- 8 @ 75%
- 7 @ 78%
- 6 @ 81%
- 5 @ 85%
- 2 @ 87%
- 2 @ 87%
- 1 @ 90%

## Reset Window (3 min)
- Strip the rack. Place wall ball, push-up spot, OH lunge space,
  and mat for V-ups all in one lane so the class starts together.

## Movement Prep (2 min)
- 5 x Wall ball shots
- 5 x Push-ups
- 5 x Alt. overhead lunges @ workout load
- 5 x V-ups

## Main Conditioning: Alternating EMOM (16 min)
**Every 2:00 for 8 sets, alternating A and B (4x each):**

- **Set A:** 15 Wall Ball Shots + 15 Push-Ups
- **Set B:** 15 Alt. Overhead Lunges (40/25 kg) + 15 V-Ups

Target: ~20-25s of rest each window. Scale load on OH lunges
or switch to knee push-ups before form breaks down.

## Cooldown & Debrief (3 min)
1. 90s easy walk, nasal breathing
2. Pigeon (deep glute): 45s/side
3. Bright spot: "I stayed consistent on the squats and kept
   the EMOM moving without stopping mid-set."
