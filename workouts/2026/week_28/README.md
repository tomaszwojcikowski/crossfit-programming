## Daily Workouts
- **[Monday](monday.md)** - Back squats 5x5 @ 80% 1RM, then 21-15-9 For Time: squat cleans and down-ups (TC: 12')
- **[Tuesday](tuesday.md)** - Team of 2: three 7'30" AMRAPs (deadlifts/push-ups/med-ball; machine/air squats/wall walks; DB snatch/T2B/power cleans) with 2'30" rest between
- **[Wednesday](wednesday.md)** - 1RM box jump height test (TC: 8'), then a descending-weight front rack lunge chipper with burpees over the bar (TC: 15')
- **[Thursday](thursday.md)** - 30' EMOM cycling 6 gymnastics and strength stations: pull-ups, pistols, T2B, HSPU, V-ups, bench/floor press
- **[Friday](friday.md)** - Team of 2: three 8' AMRAPs (row + DB snatch; AAB + wall balls; DU + KB snatch) with 3' rest between

## Lesson Planning Notes

- Monday pairs a percentage-based squat block with a fast barbell conditioning piece. Remind athletes to leave something in the tank after the 5x5—the 21-15-9 demands barbell cycling speed.
- Tuesday requires pre-staging four implements (barbell, machine, dumbbell, med ball) for every team. Announce partner split rules before each AMRAP, not mid-workout.
- Wednesday has a plyometric test followed by a barbell chipper. Protect time for the box jump test—it needs focused attempts, not a rushed clock. Load the lunges bars before the for-time starts.
- Thursday's 30' EMOM needs clear scaling paths and transition routes between stations before minute 1. Athletes must know their bench/floor press load in advance.
- Friday requires three separate implement setups per team. The 3-minute rests are built-in—enforce them so AMRAP C quality matches AMRAP A.
- Preserve stimulus by reducing load first, then volume, then complexity.

## Equipment Needs

## Focus Areas

- **Percentage-based squats into barbell cycling** (Mon): athletes need a mental reset between the two pieces—don't let the 5x5 bleed into the conditioning.
- **Partner pacing across 3 AMRAPs** (Tue): smooth handoffs and steady sets matter more than sprinting any single round.
- **Explosive box jump attempts** (Wed): controlled ramp-up, honest max, then a clean transition to the lunge chipper.
- **EMOM discipline** (Thu): finish early and reset—don't allow creeping rest that disrupts the 6-station cycle.
- **Three-AMRAP energy distribution** (Fri): effort should feel similar in AMRAP C as in AMRAP A.
