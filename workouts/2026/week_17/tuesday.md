# Tuesday (21/04/2026)

**Setup**
- Equipment: Rack, barbell, plates, kettlebell 24/16 kg,
  pull-up rig, shuttle lane marked every 8 m
- Timing: 60 min hard cap
- Stimulus: Peak back squat loading, then a short unilateral
  leg-and-pull conditioning piece where front rack posture and
  grip stay under control.
- Scaling A: Keep squat percentages if possible. Lunges with
  lighter KB. Pull-ups to banded pull-ups. Shuttle runs stay the
  same distance.
- Scaling B: Reduce squat loads 5-10% across the wave. Reverse
  lunges bodyweight or goblet hold. Pull-ups to ring rows.
- Logistics: One rack per athlete. Set the kettlebell and rig
  station next to the shuttle lane before squats begin.
- Top Cues: "Drive through the floor." "Keep the bell glued to
  the rack." "Pull elbows to ribs."

## Warmup (10 min)
- **R1 RAISE (2:00 easy):** Easy jog or bike flush
  - 8 x Glute bridges
  - 8 x Air squats
- **R2 ACTIVATE (1:30 moderate):** Shuttle rhythm build with
  relaxed turns
  - 6 x Alternating reverse lunges
  - 6 x Scap pull-ups
- **R3 POTENTIATE (1:00 hard):** 2 x 15s acceleration shuttles
  - 4 x Jump squats
  - 4 x Kip swings or ring rows
- Rest 30s between rounds

## Post-Warmup Mobility (90s)
- Couch stretch: 45s/side
- Lat stretch on rig: 45s/side

**Every 2:00 for 7 sets:**

- 6 @ 78%
- 5 @ 81%
- 4 @ 85%
- 3 @ 87%

- Strip the rack down and stage the kettlebell, rig lane, and
  shuttle turn lines so the class can start together.

## Movement Prep (3 min)
- 5 x Front rack reverse lunges/side @ workout load
- 3 x Pull-ups or scaled version

**Time cap:** 13 min (expected: 8-11 min)
