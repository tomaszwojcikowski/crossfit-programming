# Thursday (07/05/2026)

**Setup**
- Equipment: Wall space for HSW, medball 50/30 kg, pull-up rig or wall for HSPU, cones or tape at 6m for shuttle, mat for plank
- Timing: 60 min hard cap
- Stimulus: 25-minute EMOM density. Five very different demands each minute—keep quality through all five rounds, not just the opening set.
- Scaling A: HSW to 3 wall-facing shoulder taps + 5m bear crawl. Medball to 40/25 kg. HSPU to 6-8 box pike push-ups. Shuttle as written.
- Scaling B: HSW to 2 wall walks. Medball to 30/20 kg. HSPU to hand-release push-ups. Shuttle to 10 line hops or fast feet.
- Logistics: One wall/rig lane per athlete. Cones set at 6m before class. Medball and mat staged at each lane. HSPU athletes confirm head clearance.
- Top Cues: "Balance, then walk." "Drive the ball down fast." "Kick to the wall first, then invert."

## Warmup (10 min)
- **R1 RAISE (2:00 easy):** Easy jog or bike flush
  - 8 x Glute bridges
  - 8 x Cat-cows
- **R2 ACTIVATE (1:30 moderate):** Shuttle run build-up or direction changes
  - 6 x Kip swings
  - 6 x Wall-facing shoulder taps
- **R3 POTENTIATE (1:00 hard):** 2 x 15s shuttle acceleration burst
  - 2 x Wall walks
  - 3 x Medball squat + overhead toss (light)
- Rest 30s between rounds

## Post-Warmup Mobility (90s)
- Wrist extension rocks: 45s
- Shoulder overhead stretch on rig: 45s/side

## Movement Prep (4 min)
- 2 x 5m Handstand walk or scale
- 5 x Medball over the shoulder @ workout load
- 4 x HSPU or scale
- 2 x 6m shuttle run
- 30s plank hold

## Main Conditioning: 25' EMOM
**5-station cycle (5 rounds total):**

- **Minute 1:** 2x5m Handstand Walk
- **Minute 2:** 8 Medball over the Shoulder (50/30 kg)
- **Minute 3:** 10 Handstand Push-Ups
- **Minute 4:** Max Shuttle Run (1 rep = 6m)
- **Minute 5:** 30" Plank (rest remaining 30")

**Target:** finish minutes 1-3 in under 50s each; log shuttle reps each round to track output across 5 rounds.

## Cooldown & Debrief (3 min)
1. 60-90s easy walk, nasal breathing
2. Child's pose: 45s
3. Wrist stretch: 30s/side
4. Bright spot: "I kept handstand quality through all five rounds, not just the first two."
