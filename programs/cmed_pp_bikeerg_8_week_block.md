# CMED-PP: Continuous Time Crunch Training Cycle

## Revised No-Running 8-Week Block

This block develops aerobic capacity while maintaining overhead strength,
weighted pulling strength, and power. Running is excluded. The BikeErg is the
primary conditioning tool because it creates little impact and less upper-body
fatigue than rowing or an air bike.

Every session fits within 60 minutes.

## 1. Baselines and Targets

### Conditioning

- Primary modality: BikeErg
- Week 1 baseline: 20-minute average power
- Week 8 target: improve average power with even pacing
- Pacing standard: second-half average power no more than 3% below the first
  half
- Current aerobic background: mostly 15-30-minute runs before this block

Use the same machine and damper setting for both benchmark sessions. Record:

- Average watts
- Total calories or distance
- Average and maximum heart rate, if available
- First-half and second-half average watts
- Session RPE

### Strength

- Weighted ring pull-ups: 3 reps at +16 kg
- Overhead press reference: 70 kg
- Power clean reference: 90 kg

These are reference numbers, not mandatory training loads. Daily RPE and
movement speed determine loading.

## 2. Weekly Architecture

- Monday: Push, 20-25 min; easy BikeErg, 12-18 min
- Tuesday: Quality BikeErg, 25-40 min
- Wednesday: Pull, 22-25 min
- Thursday: Easy BikeErg, 20-30 min
- Friday: Legs, 22-28 min
- Saturday: Rest
- Sunday: Long BikeErg, 30-50 min

On Monday, complete strength first. Take 3-5 minutes to transition, then ride
easy. The combined session should take approximately 35-48 minutes.

## 3. Conditioning Guidelines

### Easy BikeErg

- RPE 2-3
- Fully conversational breathing
- Smooth cadence, normally 80-95 RPM
- Light enough that the legs feel better after the ride
- Do not chase watts when fatigued

### Long BikeErg

- RPE 2-3 throughout
- Finish feeling able to continue another 10-15 minutes
- Keep power steady rather than increasing effort late
- Stand briefly or change hand position as needed for comfort

### Threshold Work

- RPE 6-7
- Strong but controlled breathing
- Finish each interval knowing another interval would be possible
- Avoid a large power surge at the start

### Strong Intervals

- RPE 7-8
- Repeatable output, never an all-out sprint
- Keep interval power within 5% from first repetition to last
- Reduce target watts if cadence or posture deteriorates

### Alternative Machines

A Concept2 rower or air bike may replace the BikeErg when necessary:

- Keep all work and recovery durations unchanged.
- Use RPE and repeatable output rather than converting watts, metres, or
  calories.
- Use the same machine for both benchmark sessions.
- Expect more upper-body fatigue from rowing and the air bike.

Do not compare output numbers between different machine types.

## 4. Strength Protocol

### Day 1: Push

**Time: 20-25 minutes**

#### Warm-up: 3 minutes

- 30-45 seconds easy BikeErg or brisk movement
- 8 scapular push-ups
- 10 band pull-aparts
- 5 empty-bar presses
- 2-3 short press ramp-up sets

#### Overhead Press

- 1 x 3-5 reps at RPE 8
- Stop the set if the bar path or speed changes noticeably

#### Quality-Density Block: 8-10 minutes

Complete controlled rounds of:

- 3 overhead presses at 70-75% of the top-set load
- 8-12 ring push-ups or incline dumbbell presses

Maintain at least two reps in reserve. Rest as needed rather than rushing.

#### Shoulder Health

- Face pulls or band external rotations
- 1-2 x 12-15 controlled reps

### Day 2: Pull

**Time: 22-25 minutes**

#### Warm-up: 3 minutes

- 30-45 seconds easy BikeErg or brisk movement
- 10 band pull-aparts
- 6-8 ring rows
- 3 scapular pull-ups
- 1-2 short pull-up ramp-up sets

#### Weighted Ring Pull-ups

- Top set: 1 x 3-5 reps at RPE 8
- Back-off: 2 x 3-5 reps with 1-2 kg less than the top set
- Alternate neutral grip and free rotation each week

Keep every repetition strict. End the set before range or control changes.

#### Quality-Density Block: 8-10 minutes

Complete controlled rounds of:

- 8-12 one-arm dumbbell rows per side
- 8-12 hammer curls

Maintain one or two reps in reserve on both movements.

### Day 3: Legs

**Time: 22-28 minutes**

#### Warm-up: 4 minutes

- 60 seconds easy BikeErg
- 8 bodyweight good mornings
- 6 alternating reverse lunges
- 8 light kettlebell deadlifts
- Empty-bar clean rehearsal

#### Hang Power Clean

- 5 x 2 reps at 45-60 kg
- Start a set every 60-75 seconds
- Use approximately 50-65% of the power-clean reference
- Stop adding load if the pull or catch slows

#### Trap-Bar Deadlift or Barbell RDL

- Top set: 1 x 3-5 reps at RPE 7-8
- Back-off: 2 x 3-5 reps at 90-95% of the top-set load
- No misses, grinders, or slow final repetitions

Use one hinge variation for the full eight-week block.

#### Accessory Block

Start one round every 2:30 for 3 rounds:

- 8-12 kettlebell swings
- 20-30-second Spanish squat or wall sit
- 10-15 calf raises with a pause or slow lowering phase

Finish each round with enough time to breathe and reset.

## 6. Strength Progression

Use small, movement-appropriate increases:

- Overhead press: add 1-2.5 kg
- Weighted ring pull-up: add 0.5-1 kg
- Deadlift or RDL: add 2.5-5 kg
- Hang power clean: add weight only while speed and catch quality remain stable

Increase a main lift after two consecutive sessions completed at RPE 7 or
lower with consistent speed. Hold the load after an RPE 8 session.

Reduce the load by 5-10% when:

- The prescribed minimum reps are not available
- Bar or body speed changes substantially
- Technique or range deteriorates
- Warm-up loads feel unusually heavy

Do not progress load and density-round count in the same session.

## 7. Fatigue Rules

### Conditioning

- If interval power falls by more than 5%, reduce the target by 3-5%.
- If power continues to fall, stop one interval early.
- Never turn an easy ride into compensatory hard work.
- If Sunday feels flat for two consecutive weeks, reduce Friday accessories
  before reducing conditioning volume.
- If fatigue remains, shorten Thursday by 20-30%.

### Strength

- Friday remains submaximal throughout the block.
- No missed repetitions or grinders.
- End a density block early when repetition quality changes.
- Pain that changes movement is not ordinary fatigue; stop or substitute the
  affected movement.

### Recovery

- Keep Saturday free from structured training.
- Light walking and relaxed mobility are acceptable.
- Resume normal loading only when warm-ups feel coordinated and crisp.

## 8. Session Tracking

After each session, record:

- Session duration
- Session RPE from 1-10
- Main-lift load, reps, and RPE
- Interval or benchmark watts
- Any output fade across intervals
- Notes on sleep, soreness, and unusual fatigue

The block is successful when BikeErg benchmark output improves without
degrading strength performance, movement quality, or recovery.
